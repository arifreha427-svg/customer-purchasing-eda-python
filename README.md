# Customer Purchasing Pattern Analysis
## 📊 Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a retail customer purchasing dataset containing over 10,000 records. The objective is to uncover purchasing patterns, customer behavior trends, payment preferences, and sales insights that can support business decision-making.
Using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, the dataset was cleaned, analyzed, and visualized to identify meaningful trends and actionable insights.

---
##  Objectives

* Analyze customer purchasing behavior.
* Identify high-performing product categories.
* Explore age and gender-based shopping trends.
* Investigate payment method preferences.
* Detect outliers in customer spending.
* Generate business insights through data visualization.

##  Key Insights

### Sales Trends

* Certain product categories generated significantly higher revenue than others.
* Monthly sales patterns revealed seasonal purchasing behavior.
* Peak shopping periods were observed during specific months.

### Customer Behavior Insights

* Middle-aged customers contributed the highest number of purchases.
* Male and female customers showed slight differences in purchasing patterns.
* High-frequency customers demonstrated repeat buying behavior.

### Payment Method Analysis

* Credit cards were the most commonly used payment method.
* Online payment methods were used more frequently than cash transactions.

### Outlier Detection

* Several extreme purchase values were identified.
* These outliers may represent premium customers, bulk purchases, or data anomalies.

### Correlation Findings

* Quantity purchased showed a positive relationship with total spending.
* Some variables exhibited weak correlations, indicating independent customer behavior factors.

### Shopping Mall Performance

* A small number of shopping malls consistently generated the highest transaction volumes.
* High-performing locations present opportunities for targeted marketing and inventory optimization.

---

##  Final Conclusion

The exploratory data analysis successfully identified customer purchasing patterns, seasonal trends, high-performing product categories, and spending outliers. These findings can help businesses improve marketing strategies, optimize inventory management, and make more informed, data-driven decisions.
